Cardinal Directions

Sahven is a tidally locked planet distinct from Earth, so the conventional cardinal directions hold no relevance. Despite the existence of north and south poles, the inhabitants rely on alternative navigational references. Rather than utilizing the notions of "North" and "South," they orient themselves using "Dayward" and "Nightward" on their maps. These terms correspond to "Up" and "Down" respectively, based on the fixed position of the sun in the Dayward sky.

Furthermore, Sahven's massive magnetic field generates a vast aurora that traverses the heavens from the planet's southern pole to its northern pole. This remarkable phenomenon gives rise to two additional directions known as "Ebb" and "Flow." In relation to the Dayward orientation, the directions of Flow, Nightward, and Ebb are situated at 90-degree intervals in a clockwise manner.

Sahven Peninsula

The majority of Sahven is covered in a high acidity sea, with the Sahven Peninsula being the only habitable land on the entire planet.
